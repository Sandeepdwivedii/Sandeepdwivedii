LINEAR ALGEBRA

Course credit : 4

Linear Algebra is a fundamental branch of mathematics that studies vector spaces and linear mappings between these spaces. It plays a crucial role in various fields, including physics, computer science, and engineering.

ENGINEERING PHYSICS

Course credit : 4

Engineering Physics explores the principles of physics applied to engineering problems, providing a foundation for various engineering disciplines. This course covers topics such as mechanics, thermodynamics, and electromagnetism.

FUNDAMENTALS OF ELECTRICAL AND ELECTRONICS

Course credit : 4

This course covers the fundamental concepts of electrical and electronics engineering, including circuits, signals, and systems. You will gain insights into the principles underlying electronic devices and their applications.

PROBLEM SOLVING WITH PROGRAMMING

Course credit : 5

Problem Solving with Programming introduces students to the art of programming, helping them develop problem-solving skills using coding. The course covers algorithmic thinking, data structures, and practical coding techniques.

DISCRETE MATHEMATICS

Course credit : 4

Discrete Mathematics is the study of mathematical structures that are fundamentally discrete rather than continuous. Topics include logic, set theory, combinatorics, and graph theory.

DATA STRUCTURES AND ALGORITHMS

Course credit : 4

Data Structures and Algorithms is a foundational course in computer science, covering the design and analysis of algorithms and the implementation of essential data structures for efficient computation.

WEB DEVELOPMENT

Course credit : 2

Web Development introduces the principles of creating dynamic and interactive websites. Topics include HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and server-side scripting languages for building modern web applications.

COMPUTER ORGANIZATION AND ARCHITECTURE

Course credit : 4

Computer Organization and Architecture explores the design and organization of computer systems, including topics such as CPU design, memory systems, and instruction set architecture.

PRINCIPLES OF DATA COMMUNICATION

Course credit : 3

Principles of Data Communication covers the fundamentals of transmitting data across networks. Topics include protocols, data encoding, and the design of communication systems.
